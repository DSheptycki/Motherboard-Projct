Form Factor

What is Form Factor

Form Factor is the size and avaliable components that belong to a motherboard. Selecting Form Factor is very important because of compatibility and what's ideal, you don't want to purchase / use a motherboard that has tons of extra unused components or doesn't fit into your case.

ATX Form Factor

The characteristics of a ATX form factor, replacing AT form factors has; Intergrated I/O port connectors, Intergrated PS/2 mouse connector, Reduced drive bay interference, Reduced expansion card interference, better power supply connector, “Soft Power” support, 3.3V power support, better air flow and Improved upgradability.

Micro ATX

The characteristics of a Micro ATX form factor are identical to the normal ATX, but the micro ATX is 25% smaller compared to the typical 244 x 244 mm ATX. It supports all the same as ATX motherboards but typically cuts back on a few extra components to save space.

ITX Form Factor

The ITX form factor is a style of small motherboard that is often used in low cost small setups, such as small computers and other things along the lines as it is cost effective, and compatible with a lot of components.

Mini ITX Form Factor

The Mini ITX form factor is nearly identical to the regular ITX but with some differences, the Mini ITX measures 17 cm x 17cm (6.75” x 6.75”). usually has two memory slots, 1 PCI Express or mini-PCI slot, several USB ports, and one network port. Occasionally a Mini-ITX will include a onboard VGA or HDMI port.
